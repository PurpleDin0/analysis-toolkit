# The AI Contamination Problem

## The Counterintuitive Problem

Most people assume that if you give an AI your best writing samples, it will write like you. That is not what happens. The AI correctly identifies your patterns, then injects its own default habits into the output. Your clean, authentic voice comes back contaminated with phrases and structures you never used.

## How It Works

### You Provide Clean Samples

Your writing is authentic, direct, and free of AI patterns:

> "The experiment tested three variables across five trials. Temperature emerged as the dominant factor, influencing outcomes 40% more than pressure or time."

### The AI Analyzes Correctly

The AI identifies your patterns accurately: direct statements, specific data, active voice, clear cause-effect relationships.

### The AI Writes "In Your Style"

But when the AI generates text using your style, it injects its own defaults:

> "It's worth noting that the experiment examined a pivotal set of variables—temperature, pressure, and time—across multiple trials, highlighting the transformative nature of temperature's impact. Moreover, the results served as a testament to temperature's groundbreaking significance, demonstrating its influence on outcomes (a remarkable 40% more than pressure or time), thereby showcasing the robust relationship between thermal conditions and experimental results."

### The Contamination

Every one of these patterns was injected by the AI. None appeared in the original:

- "It's worth noting that" — padding opener
- "pivotal set" — importance puffing
- Em dashes — not in the original
- "highlighting the transformative nature" — trailing participle plus generic superlative
- "Moreover" — formulaic transition
- "served as a testament to" — importance puffing
- "groundbreaking significance" — generic superlative
- "demonstrating," "thereby showcasing" — trailing participles
- "remarkable" — unnecessary intensifier
- "robust relationship" — marketing language

Two sentences became one bloated paragraph. The data stayed the same. The voice changed completely.

## Why This Happens

AI models develop default writing habits from training on billions of words: em dashes for asides, groups of three, "It's not just X, it's Y" contrasts, importance puffing phrases, trailing participles, generic superlatives. These patterns activate by default.

A style guide that only describes what TO do leaves gaps. The AI fills those gaps with its own tendencies. Without a rule against em dashes, the AI adds them. Without a rule against "pivotal," the AI uses it. Without a rule against trailing participles, every sentence grows a dangling clause.

The formula: **your style guide + AI defaults = contaminated output.**

## The Fix: Explicit Negative Constraints

Positive rules alone are not enough. You need explicit bans:

- **Do not use** em dashes as default separator
- **Do not use** "pivotal," "significant," "notable" as filler adjectives
- **Do not use** trailing participles ("highlighting," "demonstrating," "reflecting")
- **Do not use** "It's worth noting," "It is important to recognize"
- **Do not use** "moreover," "furthermore," "additionally"
- **Do not use** "groundbreaking," "transformative," "cutting-edge"

Then test. The AI writes a sample in your style. You identify contamination. You add explicit bans. The AI rewrites. You check again. Repeat until the output matches your actual voice.

## A Longer Example

### Student's Original (Thesis Introduction)

> "Cybersecurity protocols in healthcare organizations must balance patient data protection with operational efficiency. Current frameworks fail on both counts. This research examines three hospitals that implemented zero-trust architectures, measuring impact on both security metrics and clinical workflow times."

Clean. Direct. Authentic voice.

### AI Output Without Negative Constraints

> "It is worth noting that in today's increasingly complex digital landscape, cybersecurity protocols in healthcare organizations face a pivotal challenge—balancing patient data protection with operational efficiency. Moreover, current frameworks not only fail to address security concerns, but they also fall short in maintaining operational effectiveness. This groundbreaking research examines three notable hospitals that have implemented transformative zero-trust architectures, thereby demonstrating the robust potential of these innovative systems through rigorous measurement of both security metrics and clinical workflow times, highlighting the significant impact on healthcare delivery."

Contamination injected: "It is worth noting that," "increasingly complex digital landscape," "pivotal challenge," em dashes, "Moreover," "not only... but also," "groundbreaking," "notable," "transformative," "thereby demonstrating," "robust potential," "innovative," "rigorous," "highlighting the significant impact." Fourteen AI patterns in a single paragraph. None appeared in the original.

### After Applying Negative Constraints

> "Cybersecurity protocols in healthcare organizations must balance patient data protection with operational efficiency. Current frameworks fail on both counts. This research examines three hospitals using zero-trust architectures, measuring impact on security metrics and clinical workflow times."

The authentic voice returns. Contamination eliminated.

## Common Misconceptions

**"My writing is good, so I don't need negative constraints."** Your writing may be excellent. The AI will still contaminate it. Negative constraints are not about fixing your writing. They are about preventing the AI from polluting your voice.

**"If I give the AI good examples, it will naturally avoid bad patterns."** It will not. AI default tendencies activate unless explicitly banned. Good examples tell the AI what to do. They do not tell it what not to do.

**"AI contamination means checking if I write like an AI."** The framing is backwards. This is about preventing the AI from writing like an AI when it is supposed to write like you.

**"Negative constraints are optional."** Without them, the AI will contaminate your voice every time. They are mandatory.

## The Meta-Lesson

Building a style guide with negative constraints teaches you that AI tools have inherent biases, that good inputs do not guarantee good outputs, that tools require active management through explicit constraints, and that testing reveals problems that assumptions miss.

These are AI literacy skills that apply far beyond writing.

## Bottom Line

Your authentic voice + AI assistance without negative constraints = contaminated output.

Your authentic voice + AI assistance with explicit bans = authentic voice preserved.

The negative constraints are not about detecting AI in your writing. They are about preventing AI from contaminating your writing.

---

**For students:** When you build your style guide, the "Do Not Use" list is the most important section. That is what keeps your voice authentic.

**For instructors:** Help students understand this is not about their writing quality. It is about tool management and AI literacy.
